Do opioid overdoes tend to be associated with less affluent areas—that is, areas where families have lower incomes?¶

The answer to this is complicated but, according to the data, there seems to be some correlation of median household income to opiod overdoses in Virginia where a the most overdoses did occur in the lower median income bands but this doesn't tell a complete story. Many counties in that same income bracket also reported zero overdoses.
